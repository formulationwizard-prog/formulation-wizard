# Competitor note — CogniLens (2026-06-08)

Source: operator-shared Indeed listing ("Regulatory Expert — Food, Supplements & Cosmetics") →
cognilens.ai. First-pass intel; not verified against primary funding/team records.

## What they are
- Berlin startup (CogniLens UG). AI **predictive regulatory intelligence** platform.
- Origin sector: **pharma + medical devices** (Class II/III, 510(k)/PMA, MDR, EMA/ICH/ISO).
- Now listing **food supplements (EFSA notifications) + cosmetics (EU 1223/2009)** as target audiences.
- Stage: open **seed** round for 2026. Team size undisclosed. Built "with an advisory board of regulatory/HTA experts."
- Hiring posture (the tell): seeking a former-regulator/EFSA-FDA advisor, **equity comp, few hours/month** —
  i.e. still *acquiring* the regulatory brain that defines the rules. Pre-product in our sector.

## What they do
- Ingest finished documentation (protocols, DHFs, CERs, **labeling**, submissions).
- Rank regulatory risks by severity against FDA/EMA/MDR/**EFSA**/HTA; explain why; suggest mitigation.
- Claim: "10× faster risk ID," "30–50% less rework." Also claim to revise → return submission-ready packages.

## Wedge difference (why we are not the same product)
- **They audit at the end.** Risk-detection gate on *already-drafted* documentation, pre-submission.
- **We create at the start.** Formulation-first workspace — operator builds the product and compliance is
  generated as they go. We own the *creation surface*, not just the final audit. Stickier wedge.
- **Geography/regime:** they're EU-first (EFSA, 1223/2009). We're US-first (FDA, 21 CFR 111/101, FALCPA, DSHEA).
  Limited head-on overlap *today*; convergence risk later.

## Where they have edge we must respect
- Pharma credentialing + EU-regulator advisory connections = real **authority moat** on the EU side.
  This is exactly the authority our **PA-pilot track** is meant to establish on the US/FDA side. Move that forward.

## Where our architecture already answers their open problem
- Their "embed global compliance logic into the framework" = our **rules-in-code-need-CFR-citation** discipline
  (Rules Registry). They're staffing it as a *person*; we treat it as *architecture*. Watch which scales.
- Their platform implicitly *is* the expert (encodes rules, returns "fixed" docs). Our **honest-engine doctrine**
  deliberately does the opposite: surface uncertainty + confidence + range, route determinations to PA.
  Bet: when their model is wrong on a borderline call, "we fixed it for you" is a liability; "here's our
  confidence and who must verify" is the dignity+defensibility moat.

## Read
Market is **validating the thesis** (multiple teams now see AI regulatory intelligence for food/supplements
as a category) — but no one in view yet owns the formulation-first creation surface for US supplements.
That's still open. Move the PA-authority track; it's the one axis where an EU-credentialed competitor is ahead.
