## What it is

**Formulation Wizard is a verification engine for regulated consumable products** — starting with dietary supplements, then food & beverage, then cosmetics and pet.

It's not a database. Databases compete on completeness; we compete on **honesty**. The platform tells operators exactly what's verified vs. what still needs supplier confirmation, then enforces FDA-correctness (FALCPA, 21 CFR 101.36, cGMP) by construction.

In plain language: a supplement brand pastes their formula, and the platform shows them every misbranding, allergen, or compliance gap on their label — before an auditor does.

**Where we sit:** we're not the auditors (NSF, USP — we feed them), and we're not the compliance databases (they compete on data completeness; we compete on honest correctness). We're the layer *upstream* of the audit, where neither plays.

---

## Why now

**Amazon's 2026 cGMP mandate** expanded third-party verification to **every dietary supplement sold on Amazon** (sources: NutraIngredients, Nutraceuticals World, Dec 2025).

- Sellers must pass a third-party audit (NSF, USP, BSCG, etc.)
- 90-day clock once Amazon contacts you
- Non-compliance = delisting
- **The verification covers label accuracy** — FALCPA, 21 CFR 101.36 — which is our core

Every supplement brand on Amazon is now budgeting for compliance, on a deadline, under threat of losing their listing. The market we serve just got created — with urgency, money, and a clock.

We're not the auditor. **We're the upstream tool brands need to get audit-ready** — the layer that catches what would get them delisted *before* they spend on the audit. Complementary to the auditors, not competing with them.

---

## What's working today

Six months of build, multiple architecture iterations, regulatory-depth correctness:

- Paste any supplement formula → instant Supplement Facts panel + safety analysis + allergen detection + verification report
- FDA-format compliance: §101.36 byte-faithful regulated outputs, FALCPA Top-9 allergen handling with species-naming, 21 CFR 111 cGMP framework
- **Honest verification by construction:** when we can't confirm something, we say so plainly — *"Contains: Milk — catalog default, confirm with supplier COA before label print."* Operators never get a false all-clear.
- ~380 ingredients catalogued — the top 100 with verified provenance, the rest **explicitly flagged as unverified rather than faked.** The honesty is the product.
- 1,500+ tests passing; harm-critical dose and labeling math verified across the label, safety, and cost surfaces — with a known, tracked punch-list to launch.

**The edge today** is regulatory-correctness depth plus operator trust — hard to replicate, real now. **The durable moat** is the predicted-vs-measured verification corpus that accumulates as operators use it: the thing database competitors structurally can't copy. That moat is a multi-year build — it doesn't exist yet, and I won't pretend it does. Today's correctness is what earns the usage that builds it.
